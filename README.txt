WATER POLO WHITEBOARD
=====================

A simple drag-and-drop tactics board for water polo. Everything lives in one
file. No internet, no install, no accounts. Your work saves automatically.

The only file you need is:  whiteboard.html
(The "server.js" file in this folder is just a helper used during testing. You
can ignore or delete it — you do not need it to use the whiteboard.)


HOW TO OPEN (on your Mac)
-------------------------
Double-click  whiteboard.html.  It opens in your web browser (Chrome or Safari).

Want it one click away?
  • Mac Dock: drag whiteboard.html onto the right side of the Dock.
  • Or in your browser: File > Add to Dock (Chrome) / drag the tab to make it
    its own window, then add to Dock.


USE IT ON YOUR iPHONE (or any device) — the web link
-----------------------------------------------------
The whiteboard is also live on the web at:

    https://matthewjfarmer12-gif.github.io/water-polo-whiteboard/

On your iPhone:
  1. Open that link in Safari.
  2. Tap the Share button (the square with the up-arrow).
  3. Tap "Add to Home Screen."
  4. Now it has its own app icon and opens full-screen like an app.

Notes:
  • The page is public (anyone with the exact link can open the blank board),
    but your saved boards are NEVER uploaded — they stay only on your device.
  • Each device keeps its OWN boards (Mac and iPhone are separate). To copy
    boards between them, use Backup on one device and Restore on the other.
  • To update the web version later, just ask Claude to publish the change.


THE BASICS
----------
PIECES (left side, under "Pieces"):
  • Drag any piece onto the court — Offense (blue O), Defense (red X),
    Goalie (G), Ball, Goal, Cone, or Text.
  • Or just click a piece and it drops in the middle of the court.
  • Sizes stay consistent, like a real magnet board.

MOVE THINGS:
  • With the "Select" tool, drag any piece to reposition it.

NUMBERS / LABELS:
  • Double-click a player to set or change its number/label.
  • Offense numbers 1–7. Number 7 automatically wears a goalie cap
    (for 7-on-6 / pulled-goalie situations).
  • Defense numbers 1–6. (Tip: double-click and type "X3" if you want the
    letter shown too.)
  • Double-click a Goal piece to rotate it 90 degrees.

DRAW ARROWS & LINES:
  • Pick Arrow, Line, Dash Arrow, Dash Line, or Pen, then drag on the court.
  • ASSISTED STRAIGHT LINES: Arrow/Line tools always draw a perfectly straight
    line from where you press to where you release.
  • Hold SHIFT while drawing to lock to clean angles (straight across, up/down,
    or 45 degrees).
  • Choose a color from the Line Color swatches (or the rainbow picker).
  • Pen = freehand scribble.

DELETE:
  • Click a piece or line to select it, then press Delete/Backspace.
  • Or use the Erase tool and click things to remove them.

UNDO / REDO:
  • Buttons on the left, or Cmd+Z (undo) and Shift+Cmd+Z (redo).


BOARDS (the tabs along the top)
-------------------------------
  • You can have as many boards as you like.
  • "＋ Board" adds a new one (it starts from your default template).
  • Click a tab to switch to it.
  • Double-click a tab to rename it.
  • Click the ✕ on a tab to delete that board.

LAYOUTS (left side, "This Board"):
  • Half Court  – one goal (the standard attacking-end view).
  • Full Court  – two goals, for full-game / transition setups.
  • Blank Pool  – an empty pool; drag in Goal pieces wherever you want.


TEMPLATES & DEFAULTS
--------------------
  • Built-in starting setups are included (6v6 Front Court, 6v5 Power Play,
    Empty Half Court, Empty Full Court).
  • "Save Board as Template…" turns the current board into a reusable template.
  • "Load" drops the selected template onto the current board.
  • "Set Default" picks which template every new board starts from
    (marked with a ★).


SAVING & BACKUP
---------------
  • Your boards and templates save automatically in this browser. Close it and
    reopen anytime — it's all still there.
  • "Save Image" downloads the current board as a PNG picture (great for texts,
    printouts, or film notes).
  • "Backup" saves EVERYTHING to a file you can keep or send to another coach.
  • "Restore" loads a backup file back in (also how you move your boards to a
    different computer).

A note on auto-save: it's tied to this browser on this computer. If you clear
your browser data, use a different browser, or switch computers, use Backup /
Restore to carry your work over. Backing up now and then is a good habit.
